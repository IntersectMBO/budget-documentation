### 2. Open Discussion — Why Use the Intersect Process vs. Direct Treasury Withdrawal?

Dimitri opened a thoughtful conversation that drew contributions from most members. With no agenda items requiring decisions today, the committee used the time to think through the strategic posture of the process.

#### 2.1 Dimitri's Question

Why would a proposer choose the Intersect budget process over going directly to a treasury withdrawal? Is the only real differentiator the 100K ADA deposit, or are there others?

#### 2.2 Nico — The Process Provides Iteration and Filtering

* **Iterability:** Proposers can update their proposal during the cycle. A direct on-chain treasury withdrawal anchors to an immutable IPFS document — no back-and-forth, no refinement.
* **Volume management:** With \~14 live governance actions today (and another submitted by a Vietnamese group this morning that arguably should have gone through the process), DReps are getting overwhelmed. Many will not vote on or will reject low-information proposals.
* **Quality floor:** Many direct withdrawals provide little FTE breakdown, no cost transparency, no decomposition — "we have these three work packages and there's 5M ADA for that, 5M ADA for this, that's it." The Intersect process forces a level of disclosure that direct withdrawals don't.
* **Committee role:** The Budget Committee should advocate proactively to DReps for the process — the value proposition needs to be communicated.

#### 2.3 Kristijan — The Real Issue Is the ADA Price, Not the Process

* DReps initially objected to the process as "forcing centralization" — many didn't actually read the info action; "even a simple AI would tell you" what the process did.
* Real issue is timing and ADA price: his own company went direct because waiting until August/September would prevent delivering this year. The technical community has asked them to keep working.
* Floated a different model worth thinking about: an EU-style subsidy structure (e.g., invest 20% yourself, get 80% from treasury; half upfront, half on delivery) as an alternative ethos.
* Talked to \~30 DReps; \~20 more very vocal DReps aren't reading anything and are pushing through. The committee operates at "professional process / professional expectations" but the ecosystem doesn't yet have professional standards in place.

#### 2.4 Tooling Gaps Surfaced

The conversation moved into the persistent dashboard problem.

* **Nico:** DReps must manually review what each proposer promised vs. delivered. There is no easy dashboard. The Sundae and Savos treasury dashboards are inadequate — sometimes show withdrawals without milestones, status mismatches, etc.
* **Kristijan:** The Oversight Committee dashboard is missing at least one withdrawal entirely; was told it was "a technical thing" and couldn't be added. Their committee's full delivery is outside the dashboard.
* **Christina (visitor):** The **Sundae Treasury site has improved significantly** in the last few days. She's been giving Pi specific actionable feedback and he's been responsive — he's literally being paid to make improvements right now. Encouraged the committee to send concrete issues directly.
* **Nico:** Acknowledged the improvements are real; was venting frustration at the underlying state. Showed an example: an IoG proposal status reads "withdrawn for free milestones, evidence pending review" — which a normal user would interpret as 3M ADA paid out without evidence reviewed.
* **Kristijan:** That same milestone was actually reviewed by Eric Helms on January 14 — but the dashboard surface doesn't reflect it.

#### 2.5 Christina's Framing Pushback

**Christina** raised an important framing point: it's wrong to refer to the Intersect process as **"the budget process."** It is the **Intersect budget process** — one path among several. As someone who has personally submitted governance actions (CC, Ding Bingo), she's been frustrated to see DReps treat Intersect as the end-all-be-all. The Intersect path has clear benefits (no 100K deposit, administrative support), but it isn't the only legitimate path.

#### 2.6 Front-Running Continues

**Dimitri:** The original argument for the budget process was that it would prevent front-running of the treasury, but front-running is happening anyway via the founding entities going direct. So the coordination value is partial at best.

**Lloyd:** Yes — coordination is what the process is for, and yes, it is being undermined by participants who choose to bypass it. The DReps choose what to fund; the process provides the structured information for that choice.

#### 2.7 Intersect Acting as Administrator on Direct Withdrawals

**Nico** raised a structural concern: Intersect has approved acting as administrator for the IoG and Tweag direct treasury withdrawals — proposals running parallel to the budget process. This is a revenue stream for Intersect, but it **undermines the authority of the budget process**.

**Lloyd:** It does. An entity will react to a guaranteed revenue stream over a community-service process. That's the dynamic.

#### 2.8 Macro Context — Kristijan

Kristijan closed the strategic discussion with a wider lens:

* Forecasts about ADA price from December/January are gone — "a wind over a sand castle."
* Cardano was top-10 by some measures; on Friday someone described it as **top-15** with the framing that this was something to be praised. It's not — "we are losing." Solana, TRX, XRP, ETH ahead. The ecosystem needs a more bullish posture, not a single-parent austerity mindset.
